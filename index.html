Why Australia Isn't Building Enough Homes

Australia isn't building enough new homes to keep up with its growing population. Since 1983, the number of homes given the green light to be built has gone up and down sharply, driven by rising interest rates, financial crises, and government decisions. The government's goal is to approve at least 240,000 new homes every year, but this target is almost never reached. This visualisation explores where homes are being built, what kinds, and whether supply is keeping up with demand.

Part 1

What's Happening Across the Country

Forty years of data tell a story shaped by big events. The 2008 Global Financial Crisis (a worldwide economic crash) caused a sudden drop in new home approvals. A later resources boom gave a brief boost. During COVID in 2020–21, a government building stimulus temporarily pushed numbers up. Then from 2022, the Reserve Bank raised interest rates 13 times in a row, making home loans more expensive, resulting in approvals fell to their lowest level in decades.

Are Enough New Homes Being Built?

The government has set a goal of approving at least 240,000 new homes every year to house Australia's growing population. But in reality, this target is almost never hit. Right now, only around 160,000 homes are being approved per year, well short of what the nation currently needs.

A government scheme in 2021 briefly pushed numbers close to the target. But from May 2022, the Reserve Bank raised interest rates 13 times in a row, making it much more expensive to borrow loan to build. This hit apartment construction hard. At the current rate, Australia is on track to be short by around 80,000 homes compared to what the target requires.

Part 3

What Type of Homes Are We Building?

One of the biggest changes in Australian housing over the past 40 years is the shift away from standalone houses toward apartments and units. Back in 1983, nearly all new homes built were standalone houses. However today, apartments and units make up around 4 in every 10 new homes approved nationally. In cities like Sydney and Melbourne, the numbers are even higher!

Why Some Cities Build More Apartments Than Others

Perth and Darwin have plenty of open land, so most new homes there are still standalone houses, around 4 out of 5. Canberra is the opposite: nearly 3 out of 4 new homes approved there are apartments or units. This is largely a result of government policy, which encourages smaller, more compact housing in the capital city.

In Sydney, around 6 in every 10 new homes approved are apartments or units, because land is scarce and standalone houses have become unaffordable for most people, pushing buyers and renters toward smaller homes. A high proportion of apartments isn't automatically good or bad: it means the city is building smartly in a compact area, or it can mean people simply can't afford a standard house anymore and have no other choice.

Part 4

Is Each State Building Enough for Its Growing Population?

Simply counting how many homes a state approves is not a fair comparison, bigger and more popular states will always have bigger numbers. A better question is: did each state approve enough homes to actually house the new people moving there?

To work this out, we use a simple calculation: on average, 2.5 people live in each new home, according to the ABS. So if 10,000 new people moved to a state, it would need roughly 4,000 new homes. We then compare that figure to how many were actually approved.

A score of 1.0 means a state built exactly what it needed. Blue states built more than enough. Red states fell short.

The map above shows which states are falling short, but not by how much. The chart below puts a real number on the gap.

This chart shows every state's situation at a glance. If a dot sits above the diagonal line, that state approved more homes than its new residents needed. If it sits below, it didn't build enough to keep up.

Victoria and NSW are both building more than enough for their growing populations, they're in the clear for now. Western Australia, on the other hand, is growing so fast due to people moving there that even its large number of approvals can't keep up with demand.
